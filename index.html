Why This Matters for Nairobi

The Kenyan Digital Landscape

After visiting various business websites along Moi Avenue and in Westlands, I noticed many struggle with:

  • Poor mobile compatibility
  • Missing alt text for matatu business photos
  • No semantic structure for safari tour pages

My Personal Goal

To demonstrate how simple HTML5 improvements can help Nairobi businesses:

  1. Reach more customers through better SEO
  2. Serve users with disabilities
  3. Improve mobile experience

Nairobi-Specific Discoveries

For Local Markets

Semantic HTML works perfectly for:

  • <article> - Each vendor's profile at Maasai Market
  • <section> - Different sections like jewelry, artifacts
  • <time> - Market opening hours (important for tourists!)

Accessibility Wins

Simple changes that help:

  • Adding Swahili language attributes
  • Proper contrast for sunny outdoor viewing
  • Clear labels for mobile money payment options

Local Code Examples

Nairobi Restaurant Menu

<article class="menu-item">
  <h3>Nyama Choma Special</h3>
  <img src="nyama.jpg" alt="Grilled goat meat with kachumbari">
  <p>Tender grilled meat served with our signature 
  kachumbari and ugali</p>
  <p><strong>KSh 1,200</strong></p>
  <button aria-label="Add Nyama Choma to order">
    Order Now
  </button>
</article>

Safari Tour Section

<section id="safaris">
  <h2>Maasai Mara Packages</h2>

</section>
